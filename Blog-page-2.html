Are Eggs Gluten Free Foods?

Yes, eggs by nature are gluten free.

What do I mean when I say gluten-free? Well, In order to give the right answer, I need to tell you what gluten is. Gluten is a protein found mainly in certain grains – like wheat and rye.

So gluten-free foods are foods that do not contain any gluten.

Eggs are one of the natural gluten-free foods – which makes it safe for gluten sensitive people and those suffering from celiac disease. With the rise of gluten sensitivity and celiac disease cases, gluten-free diets have become even more popular.

Aside the fact that gluten-free diet creates safety for gluten sensitive people and people with celiac disease, it is also healthy for everyone. Studies have been carried out around the world to inform us of how healthy a gluten-free diet is.

In 2018, when the golden globes released a gluten-free menu, it became the center of attraction. In addition to that, many A-list celebrities advocate for the benefits of gluten-free dieting. You could visit a dietician to help you create a gluten-free diet plan – in order to also enjoy the benefits.

Which brings us to what this article is going to talk about. You will learn about eggs and their gluten-free nature – cross contamination and how to avoid it – gluten-free recipes for eggs – and the health benefits of eggs as a gluten-free food.

Lets dive in.

HEALTH BENEFITS OF EGG

Surely, the health benefits of eggs are tied to its gluten-free nature and being a great source of protein, vitamin, and other nutrients. Here are some of the health benefits of eggs.

EGGS HELP TO IMPROVE BRAIN HEALTH


According to clinical dietician Michelle Hoeing Bauche, Ms, RDN. Eggs have a substantial amount of Choline – a newly discovered vitamin connected to the vitamin B family. In her study, she mentioned the major health benefit of the choline gotten from eggs. Choline is great for memory health. It is also good for preventing cardiovascular disease, dementia, and early brain dysfunction. Lastly, Bauche stated that choline has to work with other vitamins to be effective.

EGGS ARE GREAT FOR WEIGHTLOSS


According to a research published in the journal of American college of nutrition in 2004, meals that contain more protein have lesser calories. Especially for lean protein food like eggs. Foods that contain excess calories are very unhealthy, and contribute to obesity and overweight problems. So obviously, eggs are great for weight loss. It is capable of filling you up without the risk of getting too much calories in your body.

EGGS HELP TO BUILD STRONGER MUSCLES


As mentioned before, eggs are filled with protein. And research has shown that protein helps to maintain and repair body tissues, which include muscles.

EGGS HELP TO IMPROVE EYE HEALTH


Eggs contain lutein – a vitamin that helps to prevent macular degeneration. Macular degeneration is what causes age-related blindness.

EGGS ARE GREAT FOR PREGNANT WOMEN


Ms Bauche also talked about the effect of choline in pregnant women. According to her study, pregnant women who take in 900mg of choline has managed to double the recommended intake. 900mg of choline can be gotten from two eggs. Another research also said that the intake of choline during pregnancy will help for fetal brain development and also avoid birth defects.

NUTRITIONAL VALUE OF EGG


Eggs are obviously highly nutritious foods. They are gluten-free; they contain protein, vitamins, and many other nutrients. Here is the nutrition value of egg, based on what it is composed of.

PROTEIN CONTENT


Eggs are amazing sources of protein. The protein in eggs are great for human growth because of the amino acids it possesses. Why is protein so good for the body? Well, aside aiding growth, it has so many other functions – including repair and development of muscle mass.

VITAMINS


  • Eggs are great sources of vitamin B12. This vitamin is good for nervous system, DNA synthesis, and formation of red blood cells.
  • Furthermore, studies have shown that eggs contain substantial amount of vitamin D – which is good for bone health and improvement of the immune system.
  • Eggs are also good sources of vitamin A; which helps to improve skin health, immune system, and vision.
  • Eggs are also great sources of vitamin E. An antioxidant vitamin. Vitamin E helps to protect our cells from getting damaged.

MINERALS


  • One important mineral that eggs contain is heme iron. Heme iron helps to prevent iron deficiency anemia.
  • Eggs are also great sources of selenium which has antioxidant properties.
  • Phosphorus is another mineral that you can get from eggs. It is great for bone and teeth health.

NUTRIENT


  • The choline in egg is part of the reasons why it is a nutritious food. Choline is very crucial for brain health and so many metabolic processes.
  • Another component that make eggs amazing are its possession of carotenoids known as Lutein and Zeaxanthin. These nutrients are found in egg yolks and are great for eye health.

ARE EGGS GLUTEN-FREE?


Yes. In its natural state, eggs do not contain any gluten. However, during the cooking process, eggs can get contaminated with gluten.

You just have to be careful not to get your egg contaminated.

Here is the composition of eggs.

NATURAL COMPOSITION OF EGGS


Eggs are laced naturally with certain nutrients that are great for human consumption. Here is an overview of the composition of eggs

EGG SHELL


This is obviously the outermost part of the egg. This part is said to be made up of calcium carbonate. It is the part that protects the components of the egg.

EGG WHITE


The egg white which is also known as albumen is the biggest portion of the egg. It is made up of 90% water. This is also the part that carries most of the egg’s protein.

EGG YOLK


The egg yolk is probably the best part of the egg. It is also probably the smallest part of the egg.


  • Egg yolk contain cholesterol. Do not be scared of the cholesterol found in egg yolks. They are dietary cholesterol which will help with several physiological functions.
  • Egg yolks also contain various vitamins, such as, vitamin D, E, A, and B12.
  • The yolks also contain essential minerals.
  • Lastly, there is choline and carotenoids.

WHY ARE EGGS NATURALLY GLUTEN FREE?


Eggs are naturally gluten free because of their biological composition and origin.

Also, the protein found in eggs are not suitable with gluten.

GLUTEN CONTAMINATION AND CROSS-CONTAMINATION


Even though the risk involved in getting eggs contaminated with gluten is low, there are still some scenarios that could lead to serious problems. Here are some scenarios that could cause cross-contamination.

CROSS-CONTAMINATION DUE TO SHARING OF UTENSILS


Most people do not know this, but it is important to clean all utensils before cooking any gluten-free food.

This is important because sometimes, residue that contain gluten could be on the utensil and then transferred to your gluten free dish. This will not end well for a gluten sensitive person.

I will advice that you keep a separate set of cooking utensils for gluten free dishes.

However, keeping your utensils clean from any gluten dish can help you minimize the risks.

PROCESSED EGG PRODUCTS


This is where I urge you to always look at the label of any food you buy before eating it.

A commercially processed egg product could contain ingredients or seasoning that have gluten in them.

For those who are gluten sensitive, or on gluten-free diets, its best if you are careful enough to read the label of whatever you eat.


EGG DISHES IN RESTAURANTS

Most restaurants do not just cook only gluten-free foods. So obviously, there is risk of gluten intake.

The best way to deal with this is by informing the restaurant about your gluten-free diet before arrival. This will help them to be more careful.


TIPS ON HOW TO PREVENT CROSS-CONTAMINATION AT HOME


  • Ensure all surfaces are clean and tidied up before you start cooking.
  • Do not use wooden utensils because they are quite hard to clean. You either use silverware or buy separate utensils for gluten-free foods.
  • Store all your gluten free foods and products in separate storage spaces.
  • It is better to not prepare gluten-free foods in the same pots as gluten filled foods. However, if you must, then you can just cook the gluten free food first and then the one with gluten later. And clean properly afterwards.
